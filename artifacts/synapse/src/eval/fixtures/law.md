# Contract Law

## Offer and Acceptance

A contract is formed when one party makes a clear offer and the other party accepts it.
The offer must be communicated, definite, and made with the intention to be bound.
Courts examine whether a reasonable person would conclude that an agreement exists.

## Consideration

Each party must give something of legal value. Consideration can be a promise, an act, or a forbearance.
Without consideration, a contract is generally not enforceable, subject to exceptions such as promissory estoppel.
Legal scholars distinguish between executed and executory consideration.

## Breach and Remedies

A breach occurs when a party fails to perform without a legal excuse.
The plaintiff may sue the defendant for damages, specific performance, or restitution.
Judges apply statutes, precedent, and the Uniform Commercial Code to resolve disputes.
